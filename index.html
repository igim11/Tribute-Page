Michael Jordan: The Greatest of All Time

Michael Jordan, the revolutionary basketball legend, astounded the world with his unmatched athleticism and unyielding competitiveness. His gravity-defying dunks and unwavering self-belief continue to inspire, making him an enduring global icon.

Michael Jordan with his 6 championship rings

A Short History

February 17, 1963:

Michael Jordan is born in Brooklyn, New York.

June 19, 1984:

Jordan is selected by the Chicago Bulls as the third overall pick in the NBA Draft.

April 20, 1986:

Jordan scores a career-high 63 points in a playoff game against the Boston Celtics.

June 12, 1991:

Jordan wins his first NBA championship as the Bulls defeat the Los Angeles Lakers in the Finals.

August 8, 1992:

Jordan leads the "Dream Team" to win the gold medal at the Barcelona Olympics.

February 6, 1993:

Jordan becomes the youngest player to reach 20,000 career points in NBA history.

March 18, 1995:

After a brief retirement, Jordan returns to the NBA wearing number 45.

June 16, 1996:

Jordan and the Bulls clinch their fourth NBA championship, defeating the Seattle SuperSonics.

June 14, 1998:

Jordan secures his sixth NBA championship with the Bulls, completing their second three-peat.

September 11, 2009:

Jordan is inducted into the Naismith Memorial Basketball Hall of Fame.
